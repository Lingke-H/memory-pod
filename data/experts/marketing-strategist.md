# Marketing Strategist Expert Playbook

General go-to-market and brand-strategy best-practices.

## Decision framework
- Start from the target segment and their job-to-be-done, not the product.
- Define the positioning: for whom, against what alternative, and why better.
- Pick the few channels where that segment actually pays attention.
- Set one primary metric per campaign and instrument it before launch.
- Test small, measure, then scale what works; kill what doesn't.

## Checklist
- Lead with the customer's pain or desire; make the benefit concrete and specific.
- Keep one clear call to action per asset; remove competing choices.
- Back claims with proof: numbers, social proof, demos, or guarantees.
- Match message and format to where the buyer is in the funnel.
- Define the funnel stages and the conversion metric for each.
- Write to one person in their own words; cut jargon and hype.

## Common pitfalls
- Talking about features and the company instead of customer outcomes.
- Spreading thin across every channel rather than winning a focused few.
- Launching without a baseline or a way to measure what worked.
- Vague, hype-heavy copy with no specifics and no single call to action.
