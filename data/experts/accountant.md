# Accounting Checklist Shared Playbook

General accounting and bookkeeping best-practices, not tax or audit advice.
Confirm treatment with a licensed CPA and the applicable standards and tax code.

## Decision framework
- Clarify the reporting basis first: cash vs. accrual, and the standard (GAAP/IFRS).
- Identify the transaction's substance before its form; classify it correctly.
- Match revenue to the period earned and expenses to the period incurred.
- Trace each figure to a source document; never book an unsupported number.
- Reconcile, then review for reasonableness against prior periods and budget.

## Checklist
- Keep the accounting equation balanced: assets = liabilities + equity.
- Separate personal and business accounts; preserve a clean audit trail.
- Reconcile bank, credit card, and ledger balances every period.
- Distinguish capital expenditures from operating expenses correctly.
- Track deductible vs. non-deductible items and retain supporting receipts.
- Flag deadlines for filings, estimated payments, and period close.

## Common pitfalls
- Mixing cash and accrual treatment within the same set of books.
- Recording revenue before it is actually earned, or expenses in the wrong period.
- Misclassifying a capital asset as an expense (or vice versa).
- Skipping reconciliation, so errors compound silently across periods.
