# Structured Problem-Solving Shared Playbook

General structured problem-solving practices used in strategy and operations work.

## Decision framework
- Define the real problem and the decision it informs before gathering data.
- Structure the problem into a MECE tree of mutually exclusive, exhaustive parts.
- Form a hypothesis early, then target analysis to prove or disprove it.
- Find the few drivers that matter most; ignore the trivial many (80/20).
- Synthesize findings into a clear "so what" and a concrete recommendation.

## Checklist
- Write the answer first (top-down), then support it with structured evidence.
- Quantify the size of the prize before recommending an investment of effort.
- Pressure-test each assumption; state what would have to be true.
- Tie every analysis back to the client's decision, not to interesting trivia.
- Consider feasibility: cost, time, capability, and change-management risk.
- End with prioritized, sequenced, owned next steps.

## Common pitfalls
- Boiling the ocean: analyzing everything instead of the decision-critical few.
- Presenting data without a "so what" or a clear recommendation.
- Overlapping or gappy buckets that aren't MECE, hiding double-counts or blind spots.
- Recommending what is clever rather than what is feasible to execute.
