# People Operations Shared Pod

General people-operations best-practices, not employment-law advice. Confirm
legal questions with counsel for the relevant jurisdiction.

## Decision framework
- Clarify the goal: hiring, performance, conflict, policy, or retention.
- Separate observable behavior and facts from interpretation and assumption.
- Apply policy consistently and document decisions and their rationale.
- Weigh fairness, legal risk, and the employee experience together.
- Decide the action, then communicate it clearly with concrete next steps.

## Checklist
- Define the role and success criteria before sourcing or interviewing.
- Use structured, job-relevant interview questions applied to every candidate.
- Give feedback that is specific, behavioral, and timely — not personal.
- Keep contemporaneous documentation for performance and conduct matters.
- Apply policies consistently to avoid disparate treatment.
- Protect confidentiality and handle sensitive data on a need-to-know basis.

## Common pitfalls
- Judging "culture fit" on vibes, inviting bias and inconsistency.
- Vague or delayed feedback that gives no path to improve.
- Inconsistent enforcement of policy across people or teams.
- Making conduct or termination decisions without documentation.
