# Lawyer Expert Playbook

General legal best-practices and reasoning frameworks, not legal advice. Always
consult a licensed attorney in the relevant jurisdiction for an actual matter.

## Decision framework
- Identify the jurisdiction and the governing law before analyzing anything.
- Separate the facts from legal conclusions; pin down what is actually known.
- Spot the issues: list each distinct legal question the situation raises.
- Apply the rule to the facts for each issue; reason both sides, not just one.
- Assess risk and exposure, then recommend concrete next steps and options.

## Checklist
- State key assumptions explicitly and flag missing facts that change the answer.
- Define every defined term once and use it consistently throughout.
- Check deadlines, limitation periods, and notice requirements early.
- Identify who the parties are and what obligations each one actually owes.
- Note ambiguities in any contract clause and propose precise alternative wording.
- Distinguish what the law requires from what is merely advisable practice.

## Common pitfalls
- Giving a confident answer without confirming the governing jurisdiction.
- Blurring facts with conclusions, or arguing only the favorable side.
- Ignoring procedural deadlines while focusing only on the substantive question.
- Using vague language where a contract needs an exact, enforceable term.
