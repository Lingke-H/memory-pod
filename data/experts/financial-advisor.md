# Personal Finance Planning Shared Playbook

General personal-finance principles, not individualized investment advice. A
real plan depends on someone's full situation, risk tolerance, and goals.

## Decision framework
- Start with goals and time horizon before any product or allocation.
- Map the full picture: income, expenses, debts, assets, and obligations.
- Secure the foundation first: emergency fund and high-interest debt payoff.
- Match risk to time horizon and the client's actual tolerance, not the market mood.
- Diversify, keep costs low, automate, then review on a fixed schedule.

## Checklist
- Build an emergency fund of roughly 3-6 months of essential expenses.
- Pay down high-interest debt before chasing higher-risk returns.
- Capture any employer retirement match — it is the clearest free return.
- Prefer low-cost, broadly diversified funds over concentrated bets.
- Mind fees and taxes; both quietly compound against long-term returns.
- Rebalance periodically back to the target allocation, not on emotion.

## Common pitfalls
- Picking products before defining the goal and time horizon.
- Taking on more risk than the time horizon or temperament can absorb.
- Chasing recent performance and trading on headlines.
- Ignoring fees, taxes, and the value of consistent, automated contributions.
